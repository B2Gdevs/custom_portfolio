# Mordred's Tale — Narrative Outline

| Field | Value |

| `thematic` | Dark; origin of Magicborn; Mordred = first. The tale is *about* Mordred — by Act II it's all about him (and his time growing up); Jack/Morgana chapters support that. |

## Tone and audience

| Field | Value |

| `themes in the text` | Morally grey choices; rash action; betrayal; submissiveness vs resistance; greed; karma; scheming. |

## Character names (Arthurian-style defaults)

Working names for drafting; swap if they clash with later canon.

| Role | Name | Note |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Subordinate** (Jack’s right-hand; Morgana’s second captor; half-sister’s biological father) | **Owain** | Arthurian knight line (Yvain / Owain); reads as competent follower, not a cartoon villain title. |
| **Half-sister** (Morgana + Owain; raised in Owain’s household) | **Enid** | From Arthuriana (e.g. Geraint’s wife): endurance under constraint; distinct from **Morgana**. |
| **Daughter** (Jack’s child; ~15 in Act III; fights beside Enid) | **Gwyneth** | Welsh/medieval feel; use **Gwyneth** in narration so “Gwen” doesn’t blur **Guinevere** if she appears. |

In docs and beats, **Subordinate / half-sister / daughter** still describe function; use **Owain**, **Enid**, **Gwyneth** in prose and dialogue.

## Timeline (canon)

| Point | What |
| --- | --- |
| **Jack's chapter = the past** | Ch2 (Jack in the lair) happens *before* Morgana's current situation. Jack's story leads up to the events of Morgana's predicament. |
| **Same Morgana** | The woman sleeping in the lair during Jack's time *is* Morgana. She is there, asleep; Jack finds her, discovers she charges relics, uses her, and will build an empire on this secret. |
| **Morgana's present** | Ch1 (birth, escape) and Ch3 (council, imprisonment) are the "current" timeline. Morgana is at the council; magic is limited (gear exists, but not free-flowing). |
| **Ch3 intercuts + re-alignment** | In Ch3 we jump between: (1) Morgana’s present (where she’s taken) and (2) Jack’s timeline (still “not the same time” as Ch2). By the end of **Act I** (last beat of Ch3), Jack re-aligns into the present and they meet again. |

So: **Jack (past)** → finds sleeping Morgana, powers relics, leaves lair, builds empire on the secret; **Morgana (present)** escapes and is now at the council, with her world powered by Jack’s empire but magic not free-flowing yet. **Ch3** intercuts both timelines and ends Act I with **Jack in the present** while **Morgana is taken** (and Mordred’s orphanage route is set for Act II). Any sibling events happen later, not at Mordred’s orphanage day.

## Magic state

| State | When | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Ch3 / Act I** | Magic has *not* kicked back to free-flowing. People have magical *gear* (relics, charged items) but it’s not like the old days yet. | Leaning: Morgana is powerless without the child (when separated). |
| **Jack's power source (Act I)** | Jack powers everything with the sleeping Morgana; empire built on this secret. After she’s taken, he uses the **half-sister** (via touch) to charge relics. |
| **Free-flowing magic returns (half-sister ~14)** | **Jack discovers Merlin** when the half-sister is still 14 (roughly when they first meet), deeper in the lair at the core of the tree. Merlin has been stopping the tree’s growth from within; he doesn’t know time has passed. When Jack opens that area, magic kick-starts globally, the tree grows again, and Jack’s empire is threatened; war and conflicts arise. Merlin thought it was “just an experiment.” |

## Jack, half-sister, empire

| Beat | Content |
| --- | --- |
| **Reveal** | Jack is revealed to have been powering everything with the sleeping Morgana and to have built an empire around this secret. |
| **Subordinate (Act I)** | We **meet Owain** (working name: the **subordinate**) in Act I — Jack’s **right-hand man**; the man the empire will use with Morgana (same method as Jack in Ch2) to father Mordred’s **half-sister** (**Enid**). **Morally grey** compared to Jack; doesn’t always believe in Jack’s methods but does as he is told. |
| **Half-sister (created later)** | Empire uses Morgana again via Owain; **Enid** is born. She is **raised by Owain’s family**. She is **not born yet** when Mordred is in the orphanage. By the time of the castle meeting she is **arranged to be married to a general** in Jack’s army. |
| **Power source until Merlin** | After Morgana is taken, Jack maintains his power by using the **half-sister** (via touch) to charge relics — same as sleeping Morgana. **When she is ~14** (around the time of the castle meeting), Jack discovers Merlin at the core; opening it kick-starts magic, the tree grows again, and magic fills the world. Jack’s empire is then threatened; war and conflicts arise. |

## Act I — Foundation (Jack in the past, Morgana in the present)

| Chapter | Summary |
| --- | --- |
| **Prologue** | Set-up; world, stakes, or frame. |
| **Ch1 — Morgana** | Morgana gives birth (Mordred), escapes the lair, reaches toward the council. *Done.* |
| **Ch2 — Jack** | **Past.** Jack in Taro, sent into the lair to die; finds sleeping Morgana; discovers she charges relics; survives; plans to keep her and emerge with power. *Done.* This leads to his empire and to Morgana's current predicament. |
| **Ch3 — Morgana Imprisoned** | **Intercut.** Morgana’s present: she’s at/near the council while magic is gear-limited. Morgana is taken and Mordred’s route to an orphanage is set. **Parallel:** Jack’s timeline leads up to re-alignment. **By end of Act I:** Jack is back in the present and meets again. *In progress.* |

**Finish Act I:** Draft Ch3 so it ends with: (1) Morgana gets taken, (2) Mordred separated and routed to the orphanage **on his first day** (Midas-amulet setup), (3) Jack re-aligns into the present and they meet again. **We meet the subordinate** in Act I (the man who will father the half-sister with Morgana). That landing sets up Act II — Mordred’s and his half-sister’s lives. Half-sister is not born yet.

## Act II — Their lives (Mordred + half-sister growing up)

| Focus | Content |
| --- | --- |
| **Mordred in an orphanage** | Act II starts with baby Mordred in an orphanage after Morgana is taken. Midas amulet scene (first day or early): harmless until he touches a man holding him — turns to gold. His “silent / hard-to-control” magicborn nature revealed. |
| **Mordred’s growth** | Act II tracks Mordred growing up: fear/avoidance, caretakers, instinctive conjuring (food/water), how Magicborn are treated. |
| **Half-sister’s life** | Intercut with Mordred: half-sister is born and **raised by the Subordinate’s family**. She is used (via touch) to charge relics for Jack. By the time of the castle meeting she is **arranged to be married to a general** in Jack’s army. |
| **Castle meeting (key scene)** | **Initial meeting:** Mordred **15**, half-sister **14**. Mordred has wandered into a hallway in Jack’s castle; she sees a boy that didn’t belong, along with her guards. **Options for the beat:** (a) conflict — she gets in trouble for Mordred’s doing, or (b) a small bit of dialogue between them before Mordred has to escape or just leaves. Pick one (or blend) when drafting. |
| **End of Act II (act break)** | **Jack discovers Merlin** at the core when the **half-sister is still 14** — roughly around the time of (or just after) the castle meeting. Opening it kick-starts magic; the tree begins to grow again; magic fills the world. Jack’s empire is threatened; war and conflicts will arise. **Merlin found = closing beat of Act II.** |

**To draft:** Beat list for Act II — orphanage opener, Midas scene, Mordred’s growth; half-sister’s upbringing and her role as power source; the castle hallway meeting (15/14); then **Jack finding Merlin at the end of Act II** (half-sister ~14).

## Act III — Fast-forward to the conflict; Mordred takes the throne

| Field | Value |
| --- | --- |
| `structure` | **Act III fast-forwards** from the end of Act II (Merlin found, magic returned) to the conflict. We jump to when the southern war is underway. |
| `takeover` | **Mordred overtakes Jack and Jack’s empire easily** and becomes the **new king**. This is the origin of Magicborn — Act III dives into how he does it. |
| `time` | Mordred has **traveled and grown older**. He and **Enid** **remeet** — along with her **15-year-old daughter** (**Gwyneth**) — in the conflict, on **opposite sides**. **Act III reveal:** **Jack is Gwyneth’s father** (Enid remains **Owain’s** child from Act I). |
| `battlefield remeet` | **Fierce battle** between Mordred and his half-sister and her daughter on the **battlefield of a southern conflict** (Mordred with the neighboring nation / friends, reluctantly; she and daughter with Jack’s empire). |
| `constraints` | Dark. Origin of Magicborn. Mordred = first. Pay off Act I and Act II; land the “first Magicborn” idea; Mordred becomes king. |

### Act III — The battle (beats)

| Beat | Content |
| --- | --- |
| **Fight with spells first** | Half-sister assumes it’s better to fight Mordred using **spells** rather than the relics on her — because **Mordred had better relics** (“she assumed”). She is **correct** about the relics, but he also has **overwhelming magical ability**. |
| **Mordred drops armor, casts** | When he has a **moment to breathe** from her spells, **he drops his armor and starts casting spells too**. **Onlookers** watch as they cast **similar spells** but **speak differently** and the **spells vary**. |
| **Half-sister’s reveal** | To break through, the **half-sister disrobes her top half** to get rid of **bark** that was **suppressing her magical ability**. On reveal: **scars all over her back and front torso like a whirlwind wrapped around her body**. She uses the **rings on her hand** (as she had been), then starts **speaking in a weird way**; her fighting changes — **fireballs and wind sickles** come after Mordred. He uses **his rings** to deflect and **maneuvers past the wind sickle**. |
| **Mordred’s scars** | When **Mordred reveals his torso** he has **more scars**, **more whirlwind scars** all over — **far more than his sister’s**. |
| **True power / tide turns** | When Mordred shows his **true power** on the battlefield he **turns the tide easily**. **Challengers** come at him and his caravan **here and there**, but the **majority of Jack’s army is destroyed** by his **final spells**. He **decimates their forces with ease** and **lets the neighboring kingdom continue its invasion**. |

### Act III — After the battle (escort + carriage)

| Beat | Content |
| --- | --- |
| **Escort to Jack’s castle** | The **neighboring kingdom’s army** (the side Mordred fought with) **escorts Mordred to Jack’s castle**, along with his **half-sister**. She has **seen his power** on the battlefield; he is **basically escorted back** as the victor. |
| **Carriage scene** | **Mordred**, **Enid**, and **Gwyneth** ride back together in a **carriage**; they **talk on the way** to the castle. |
| **Half-sister’s hatred** | **Enid** **hates Jack**, **her father Owain**, and **everyone in the empire** — she was **used as a tool** to **power magical relics** for the world (through Act II into Act III). Her hatred of Jack is sharpened once **his paternity of Gwyneth** is revealed. |
| **Revelations to Mordred** | She tells Mordred she became a **military tool** because she could **wield magic relics stronger than the average person**; she opens up **more about herself and her daughter**. **In Act III it is revealed that Jack fathered the daughter** (timing: carriage, castle approach, or another Act III beat — lock when drafting). |
