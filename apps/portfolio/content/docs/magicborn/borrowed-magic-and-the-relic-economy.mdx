# Borrowed Magic and the Relic Economy

After the Fall, relics do not stop mattering when they stop working. They become hunger made metal: goblets, crowns, rings, and pendants sold for what they once did and what buyers pray they might do again. The changed world runs on memory first, charge second. When Jack discovers that charge can be stolen from a sleeping body and stored for a while in dead objects, the economy of hope becomes an economy of controlled proof.
