Owain held the infant and tried not to think of the cell.

**Enid** — Jack had supplied the name as if naming were a kindness and not a brand. Tanya had repeated it once, voice flat, smile fixed, the way women smiled when they were deciding how long they could endure before they broke something.

**Geraint** clung to Owain’s leg with sticky fingers, too young to understand anything except that the house had a new noise.

"She’s your sister," Owain said — the lie they would raise until it hardened into truth.

Geraint looked at the bundle the way children look at weather.

Owain looked at his wife.

Tanya looked back with eyes that thanked him for nothing.

Outside, Tarro went on being Tarro: markets, bells, the guild-hall chatter of **relics** and **descents**, the clean story Owain still wore in daylight like a cloak stitched from half-truths.

Inside, the second well had a face now.

He kissed Enid’s forehead because that was what fathers did.

His hands shook only a little.
