From the castle walls, **Enid** watched the horizon **change**.

The sky was not the color of Tarro’s old bargains anymore. It was **deeper**, as if someone had added extra ink to the bowl.

She lifted her hand — no bark band — and felt power **answer** without asking her name.

Joy should have come first.

What came first was **fear**.

Not of the power.

Of what men would do with girls who suddenly had it.

Below, Jack shouted orders like a man trying to re-bottle lightning.

Below that, deeper still, a cell **still** held a woman who could not charge relics anymore — and did not need to.

The world had become a **well** again.

And Mordred — **fifteen**, broken, brilliant — was not the only one who could drown in it.
