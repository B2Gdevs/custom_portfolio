Jack took the stairs two at a time, face flushed with something that looked like triumph and felt like terror.

Owain followed because chains could be keys and keys could be habit.

"You found him?" Jack snapped at a captain.

"Slippery," the man admitted.

Jack laughed once — ugly. "Good. **Alive** means **useful**."

Torches made the descent a throat. The air thickened until it tasted like metal and wet bark — the **Tree**’s breath, wrong in a world that had promised magic was **gone**.

Mordred’s small rebellion had only done what Jack wanted anyway: opened a path downward.

At the bottom, roots threaded stone like veins through skin.

Jack pressed his palm to a seam and whispered a word that made the seam **listen**.

Stone slid.

Behind it: not treasure.

A man asleep in a cage of wood and time — **Merlin**, if stories could be true.

Jack’s mouth shaped **gratitude** and **greed** in the same breath.

Mordred, dragged in by soldiers a moment later, saw the sleeping face and felt a kinship he could not name — only **heat**, only **wrong**.

Then the world **opened**.
