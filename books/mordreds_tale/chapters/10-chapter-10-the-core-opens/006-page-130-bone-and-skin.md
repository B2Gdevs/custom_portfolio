He woke to **pain** that knew its address: **right arm** — wrong angle, wrong heat.

Mordred tried the healing syllables he’d heard in the House cellars, the ones that fixed **skin** and **bruise** and other polite hurts.

Flesh knitted.

Bone stayed **broken**.

He tried again, louder, desperate — and the magic **slid** off the break like water off wax, as if his body refused to let him **fully** fix himself.

Someone laughed in the distance — not cruel, **wild** — the world drunk on new air.

Mordred staggered up. The meadow tilted. He cradled his arm and realized, with a clarity that felt like adulthood, that he would need **another person** — **aid** — someone else’s hands, someone else’s voice, someone else’s permission.

Independence had been a story for a world without free magic.

That world was over.
